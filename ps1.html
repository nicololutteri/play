History

The birth of the PlayStation dates back to 1992. Nintendo tried to work with media magnetic, but seeing that the latter were unsafe, so there is a risk of piracy. He began to use Sony's CDROM / XA media which allowed graphics and audio compression in one device in this case a CD. A contract was therefore signed. Sony was planning to create a new know her would have been compatible with Nintendo. The contract was later canceled because it allowed Sony to be able to control all games developed for the Nintendo console. Sony, therefore, with the material who had developed, up to that point, decided to create a complete console, calling it PS1.

Launch

It was launched in Japan on December 3, 1994, in the USA on September 9, 1995, and in Europe arrived on September 29, 1995. Sales were hugely successful with around 40 million units in one year.

Hardware

5 kB L1 cache
Overall MIPS performance: 30 MIPS Geometry Transformation Engine (internal sub-processor for 3D graphics processing) Overall MIPS performance: 66 MIPS
Peak graphics rendering of 360,000 monochromatic polygons per second (180,000 with textures and shadows)
Data Decompression Engine (internal sub-processor for decompression of images and videos) Overall performance in MIPS: 80 MIPS
Compatible with MPEG-1 and H.261 formats

Graphics Processing Unit (Created by Toshiba): Peak graphics rendering equal to 4000 sprites per second
Resolutions: 256 × 224 pixels / 640 × 480 pixels
Processing: Texture mapping
Gouraud shading
16.7 million colors
